Origins CV

I am an MSc Student & AI Graduate Researcher at the Center for Research in Computing of National Polytechnic Institute (Mexico City), working under the supervision of Dr. Adolfo Guzmán-Arenas (MIT alumnus and student of Marvin Minsky).

My current research focuses on designing tiny Vision Transformer architectures capable of learning from limited labeled data in complex environments. I am investigating Entropy-based kernels for vision models to represent more accurately objects features in images. Also, I am applying Combinatorial Optimization to solve the dynamic resource allocation problem in urban mobility systems (Ecobici, CDMX).

My goal is to transition into Mathematics for my Ph.D., specifically aiming to explore Information Geometry to formalize some fundamental mechanisms of intelligence.

Research Interests: Mathematical Foundations of Artificial Intelligence | Mathematical NeuroScience | Combinatorial Optimization | Information Geometry | Data Mining | Theory of Information

Research

  • MSc Thesis A Vision Model for Object Detection in Complex Environments
    In Progress
    Trained a Self-Supervised Vision Transformer (DINOv3 S/16) on a custom dataset of 60,000 unlabeled agricultural images using High-Performance Computing clusters of CIMAT. Attention mechanisms learned autonomously robust morphological features without labels. The model obtained an accuracy of 82% in linear classification (maize/weed) using only 50 annotated samples per class.
  • In prep DASE: Data Segmentation for Demand Prediction in the Ecobici System
    A symbolic algorithm for demand prediction based on association rules (Origin Station + User Type → Destination). Currently working on strategies to reduce the combinatorial explosion of mining frequent patterns across the network.
